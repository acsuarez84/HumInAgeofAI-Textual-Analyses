About This Project

Purpose

This tracking website explores both the enhancements and limitations of Large Language Models (LLMs) in analyzing Latino women's rhetoric through textual analysis. By examining 100 works spanning a century of literature across multiple genres and theoretical frameworks, we can better understand how AI technologies engage with culturally and linguistically diverse texts.

Genres Covered

  • Poetry: Verse, spoken word, and lyrical works
  • History: Historical analyses and documentations
  • Biographies: Life stories of significant figures
  • Autobiographies: Self-narratives and memoirs
  • Diasporas: Works exploring migration and displacement
  • The Body: Explorations of embodiment and corporeality

Theoretical Frameworks

  • Grammar: Linguistic structures and conventions
  • Culture: Cultural context and production
  • Rhetorical Listening: Listening across difference
  • Multiliteracies: Multiple modes of meaning-making
  • Multimodalities: Diverse communicative modes
  • Media: Media production and circulation
  • Transfer: Knowledge transfer across contexts
  • Translingualism: Language diversity and fluidity
  • Transliteration: Cross-script representation

How to Use

  1. Explore the Library: Browse 100 curated books using filters for genre, theory, and time period
  2. Input Your Text: Enter your original writing, excerpts, or abstracts in the Analysis tab
  3. Select Books: Choose books to compare with your text
  4. Generate Analysis: Create a semantic map showing connections between your text and selected works
  5. Review History: Access previous analyses to track patterns and insights over time

Data Privacy

All data is stored locally in your browser using local storage. No information is sent to external servers. Your analyses and text inputs remain private on your device.

Technical Details

This website uses HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to create an interactive interface for exploring Latino women's rhetoric. The semantic mapping functionality demonstrates how computational analysis can reveal connections across texts, while also highlighting areas where LLM understanding may be limited or enhanced.
